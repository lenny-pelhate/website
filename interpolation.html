Interpolation Techniques in Finance

Interpolation is a method used in finance to estimate unknown values between two known values. It is widely used in yield curve estimation, option pricing, and risk management.

1. Linear Interpolation

Linear interpolation is the simplest method, where the unknown value is estimated by assuming a linear relationship between two known values.

Formula:

        y = y1 + (x - x1) * (y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1)

Example in Finance: Suppose we have interest rates for 1-year and 3-year bonds but need to estimate the 2-year rate.

        Given: (1-year, 2%) and (3-year, 4%)
        Find: 2-year rate
        Solution: 2% + (2 - 1) * (4% - 2%) / (3 - 1) = 3%

2. Polynomial Interpolation

Polynomial interpolation uses higher-degree polynomials to fit a curve through multiple points.

Example in Finance: Estimating a bond yield curve using a quadratic or cubic polynomial.

3. Spline Interpolation

Spline interpolation divides the data into smaller intervals and fits piecewise polynomials to each.

Example in Finance: The cubic spline is commonly used in yield curve modeling to ensure smooth transitions.

4. Lagrange Interpolation

Lagrange interpolation constructs a polynomial passing through all given points.

Example in Finance: Estimating missing bond prices using known market data.
